The story.

Axel was born and raised in Sweden, where he learned two useful things: that a house is a system, and that enthusiasm for a football club can survive almost anything. He moved to the United States as a teenager, launched two companies before most of his friends had finished college, and eventually built a handyman business that grew to over a thousand clients and more than 200 five-star reviews.

Now he's in real estate — and the old trade carries over. He knows how a house was built, what's going to break in year seven, and whether the pool equipment is on its last summer. When he tells a buyer something, it's because he's seen it a thousand times.

He's calm, honest, and focused on clarity. He's also, privately, the kind of Arsenal fan who treats "fourth place" as a personality trait. But that's between him and his group chat.

What he brings

A builder's-eye look at what you're actually buying. A Scandinavian bias toward doing the homework. And a willingness to say the hard thing early.

01 — Builder's Eye

Knows what's behind the wall

A thousand handyman jobs teaches you what a home actually is. What's load-bearing, what was done in a weekend, what's going to become a problem in five years. You get that read on every property he shows you.

02 — Straight talker

Says the hard thing first

Calm, honest, focused on clarity. Axel will tell you a listing is overpriced, a neighbourhood is shifting, or an offer is too soft — even when it isn't what you want to hear. Especially then.

03 — Swedish by default

Does the homework

Over-prepared by nature. Comps, permits, HOA minutes, the neighbour's side of the fence. If there's something worth knowing before you sign, he'll have found it by Thursday.
